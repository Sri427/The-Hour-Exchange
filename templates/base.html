Quick Help

How do Hour Tokens work?

Everyone starts with 10 tokens. One token = one hour of your time. When someone helps you, you hand them a token. When you help someone, you earn one back. No money ever changes hands.

How do I ask for help?

Click the gold "I Need a Neighbor" button on the Town Square board. Fill in what you need, when, and how many tokens you're offering. Neighbors near you will see it right away.

Tier 1 vs Tier 2 — what's the difference?

Tier 1 — public tasks: tutoring, errands, advice.
Tier 2 — unlocked when two neighbors vouch for you. Needed for tasks inside someone's home.

What do the badges mean?

🌱 New Member — just joined
🤝 Active Neighbor — 1+ completed swap
🛡️ Trusted Neighbor — 5+ swaps
⭐ Community Star — 10+ swaps, very reliable

Something seems wrong — how do I report it?

Click the Flag icon on any listing. We review every report within 48 hours.

The whole idea, over coffee

Here's the thing about our neighborhood: everyone has something to offer. You might be great at fixing leaky faucets, or you make incredible pie. Your neighbor might know tax prep cold. We just needed a way to connect you.

So we made Hour Tokens. One hour of your time = one token. That's it. No cash, no awkwardness, no one counting favors.

The way a swap works

1

You post what you need (or what you can do). Takes about a minute.

2

A neighbor volunteers. Tokens move into a safe holding balance so nobody forgets.

3

Once you've both said "yes, that happened" — the tokens transfer. Done.

Questions? Hit the "Need Help?" button anytime.

Staying comfortable & safe

We want every swap to feel comfortable — especially if you're new here. A few things we've built in to help with that.

Tier 1 Out in the open

Great for tutoring over video, giving advice, running errands, anything in a coffee shop or park. No need to meet anyone at home.

Tier 2 Trusted, vouched-in

For tasks inside someone's home. You get here when two Tier 2 neighbors personally vouch for you — it means your neighbors genuinely trust you.

Always meet in a public place first. Trust your instincts. Flag anything that feels off.

Being a good neighbor

Really just three things we ask of everyone here.

Show up with kindness.
Whatever's going on in your day, leave it at the door. Your neighbor took a chance asking for help.
Be there when you said you'd be there.
Someone rearranged their afternoon for this. Honor that.
If something changes, say so early.
Life happens. A heads-up a day before is far easier on everyone than a no-show.
